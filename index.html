The head trainer and manager of Bittersweet Farm, Kristen fell in love with horses and riding as a young child, and spent the next several years eventing and advancing through the levels of Pony Club. In 2008 she became the second person in the country to receive their Dressage A rating. Over the years, she rode and trained a number of challenging and unlikely horses to successful show careers, each of which taught her a great deal about horsemanship, riding, and life. Kristen’s focused career in dressage started in 2006, when her mother purchased a 6-year-old Paint horse and was further facilitated by her partnership with Diwandre, a mature schoolmistress, in 2008. Kristen has since competed to Intermediare I with Diwandre and has trained Jester from training level to schooling Grand Prix. During this time, Kristen earned her USDF Bronze and Silver medals.

In December 2010, Kristen graduated from the University of New Hampshire with a B.S. in Business Administration with a focus in Marketing and Entrepreneurship, and a B.A. in Journalism. While in school, Kristen balanced her academic commitments with training multiple horses, managing the small family farm, and volunteering as an EMT. Kristen has won several year-end awards and in 2009, Kristen was honored to receive the Dressage Foundation’s Amanda Ward Scholarship Award in addition to winning the Region 8 Young Rider Championships.

In 2010, Kristen was invited to participate in The Dressage Foundation's Olympic Dream Program for advanced young riders to further their education in a comprehensive training trip to Germany. Her journal of the experience can be read here.

"I love riding not only for the challenge of training and the thrill of competitive success, but for the unique relationships I develop with my horses. I take enjoyment in seeing their personalities develop along with their training, and knowing that they appreciate seeing me, nearly as much as I do them"

Kristen moved to Florida in January of 2011 to train young horses for Horses Unlimited and ride their stallion Pik L, and continued on to work for Danish international dressage rider Mikala Gundersen as an assistant trainer and barn manager in Wellington.

In the spring of 2015, Kristen traveled to the Netherlands with her top horse, Stella, to advance their training with international trainer Rien van der Schaft at his farm. In June of 2016, Kristen returned to start the Doctorate of Physical Therapy program at the University of Vermont. She continued to return to Holland to advance her and Stella's training between semesters for a year. Kristen maintains full training of 2-3 horses while in Vermont, and returns regularly to New Hampshire to conduct clinics.

Kristen looks forward to continuing to train horses with the goal of competing in the national young horse championships, developing horse divisions, and at Grand Prix.
